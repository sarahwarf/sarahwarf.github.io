About
I am a language educator and teacher trainer specializing in the integration of AI into university language teaching. I currently teach academic English at NYU Shanghai and am completing a PhD in Education at Pädagogische Hochschule Heidelberg (Germany), where my research examines how university faculty understand and adopt AI in their teaching. My work explores practical ways educators can use AI tools to support language learning, feedback, and assessment while maintaining human-centered classroom practices. Through teaching, research, and professional development workshops, I focus on helping instructors experiment with new technologies in ways that strengthen pedagogy rather than replace it.

Faculty Development Workshops
I offer workshops and invited talks for university teachers that can be delivered as individual sessions, short workshop series, or full professional development programs, either online or in person. These sessions are designed for instructors working in EFL, EMI, and academic English contexts and focus on practical strategies teachers can apply directly in their classrooms. My sessions generally fall into two broad areas: AI-integrated teaching and language pedagogy. Universities and departments often request a specific topic, and sessions can be adapted to local needs. These sessions were originally developed through faculty development work with universities in China and through presentations at regional and international conferences. They combine classroom experience, research-informed pedagogy, and hands-on experimentation so that teachers leave with strategies they can immediately apply in their own courses. Below is a selection of topics I regularly present.

 
1.	AI-Integrated 
These workshops explore how language educators can integrate AI into teaching and learning in thoughtful and practical ways. Sessions focus on building AI literacy for teachers and students, designing classroom activities with AI tools, and addressing emerging pedagogical and ethical questions around AI use in higher education.

• Humanistic approaches to AI in the language classroom
• AI tools for speaking practice and oral feedback
• Generative AI and language assessment
• Chatbots and conversational AI in language learning
• AI literacy for teachers and students
• Designing AI-integrated classroom tasks
• Ethical questions: academic integrity, authorship, and AI use
• Practical AI tools for materials design and lesson planning
2.	Language Teaching & Pedagogy Workshops
These workshops focus on core principles of effective language teaching informed by research in second language acquisition and classroom practice. Sessions help teachers design communicative, engaging classrooms and develop practical approaches to teaching speaking, writing, and language skills across a range of teaching contexts.

• Communicative activities for reading and writing classrooms
• Teaching speaking and interactional skills in L2 classrooms
• Fluency-building tasks for language learners
• Teaching paraphrasing, synthesis, and academic integrity
• Designing effective academic reading tasks
• Graphic organizers and scaffolding for academic literacy
• Formative assessment strategies for large language classes
• Teaching critical thinking in EFL contexts
• Integrating listening into reading-focused courses
• Building student engagement and participation in language classrooms
