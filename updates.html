New Publication: "Analyzing Movie Posters Across Cultures"

My lesson plan, "Analyzing Movie Posters Across Cultures," is officially published in New Ways in Teaching Visual Literacy, a new volume from TESOL Press.

The lesson uses visual literacy strategies to develop rhetorical L2 writing skills, guiding students to read movie posters as cultural artifacts and translate those analytical insights into structured academic writing. I offer this as a faculty development workshop for instructors interested in task-based approaches to visual literacy and L2 writing instruction.

I've brought this task to life in two contexts: with multilingual learners at NYU Shanghai, and at invited talks at universities, where it has resonated with educators looking for fresh, multimodal approaches to L2 writing.

Contributing to the New Ways in Teaching series, a staple of my practice since I started teaching nearly 15 years ago, feels like a milestone.
